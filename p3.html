Bionic Structures

The concept of biomimicry can be used in our modern world in so many ways and the most common one that exists in our daily life is Bionic structures. The main concept of Bionic structures is to apply biomimicry (biological process or structure) to buildings or any engineering constructions. Speak to the biodiversity in our modern world bionic structures can reduce the amount of energy and material used in constructions as well as reduce the land used for building towers or houses. There is some concept in progress now in China which is the “Bionic Tower” in Shanghai. The tower will be the tallest building in the world at 300 floors. The design that uses biomimicry, not just the building itself, the foundation is Inspired by the biological structure as well. The traditional foundations will be replaced by a new widespread network of anchorages that is like the tree roots. The new widespread network would help the building itself against the earthquake and strong wind. It also provides a large living space on the small amount of land required which is just about (166 m x 133 m). This is helpful to the conservation of biodiversity in our modern world since we don’t need to develop more land because of population expansion and destroy the natural habitat of the wild living animals.

Why biodiversity in cities is important

The conservation of biodiversity in a city is very important because it can impact humans in different ways. The decrease in biodiversity can lead to climate change and it can cause decrease of quantity and quality in ecosystem services (food, water supply etc.). On the other side, conservation of biodiversity in the city can cause a positive impact to our health and well being. For example (Human physical health, psychological health, Societal and cultural health, Economic health and stability, etc.).

What is biomimicry?

Biomimicry is the method of imitating aspects of the biological world in technology and design to solve human problems.
