Biomimicry in military

B-2 bomber and OFFSET

background

By definition, biomimicry is a practice learned from and mimics the strategies found in nature to solve human design challenges. It has many different implications in human society including both commercial and military implications. In fact, there are numerous military implications of biomimicry that bring success to a country’s technology development and military tactics.

B-2 Bomber

First, humans have learned a lot about aerodynamics and hydromechanics from mother nature, and we apply them to battleships and fighter jets. For example, the B-2 bomber, designed and introduced in 1989 by American military engineers, mimics the shape of falcons in order to produce the best aerodynamic performance. During the designing process, one of the problems that engineers faced was designing an aircraft that’s large enough to carry a large number of weapons, while also being fuel-efficient. Fuel-efficiency is very important in aircraft design, especially for fighter jets that are going to execute intercontinental missions. Finally, engineers found the perfect shape in the form of a peregrine falcon. As a predator, the bird needs to frequently dive into the ocean from high above to catch fish. An adult peregrine falcon can reach a maximum of 390 km/h at the moment it reaches the ocean surface. Its special aerodynamic shape provides the falcon with the ability to reach such speed and save energy for the next dive. US military engineers adapted such shape to the design of the B-2 bomber, and finally made the B-2 the most advanced and long-range bomber in the world.

OFFSET

Secondly, biomimicry also provided inspirations in military tactics that lead to success in wars and conflicts. The US Defence Advanced Research Projects Agency has developed a new tactic to allow small infantry robotic forces to execute complex missions in war zones, which is called the OFFensive Swarm-Enabled Tactics (OFFSET) program. This tactic is mimicking a common behavior in both human and animal societies. Certain species of fish and birds will usually hunt and travel together to avoid predators and other dangers. When the population density reaches a certain level, individual thought will be replaced by collective consciousness and the whole population will work together to achieve a certain goal. For example, certain species of fish will form a spherical shape when the whole population is under attack by predators. In the OFFSET program, more than 250 small weaponized drones were programmed with the swarming behavior and they will collaborate together to complete missions. During the development process of the swarm model, developers tested the swarming behavior of robots using technologies such as virtual reality. During the swarm tactics game, players would use the interface to rapidly explore, evolve, evaluate swarm tactics to see which would work the best. Then, the optimal swarm tactic will be used in real-life missions and combat.

What is biomimicry?

Biomimicry is the method of imitating aspects of the biological world in technology and design to solve human problems.
